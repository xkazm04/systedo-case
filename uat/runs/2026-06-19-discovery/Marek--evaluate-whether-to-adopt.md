# Journal — Marek, "evaluate whether to adopt"

_First-person, in-character. Public marketing surfaces (`/`, `/cena`, `/clanek`). A prospect, not a user._

**Why I'm here:** I run an e-shop and I'm shopping for something to make my ad spend work harder. A link dropped me on Adamant. I've got a couple of minutes to decide if it's worth a closer look.

**Homepage.** "Stand adamant. Ads that never crack. Adamant forges your advertising into one unbreakable monument — watched, defended and optimised by AI around the clock." Okay… that's a lot of monument. *What does it actually do?* I scroll: three cards — Performance dashboard, Campaign intelligence, AI ad studio — and "Works across Google Ads, Sklik, Meta, TikTok." *Now* I get it: AI that watches your ad performance, triages campaigns, and writes the ads, across the channels I use. The cards did the work the headline didn't. (→ MB2) But two things nag me: it never says it's **for an e-shop like mine** (or for agencies?), and — more importantly — **there's no evidence anyone uses it or that it works.** No results, no "we cut PNO by X", no logos, no testimonials. Just claims. (→ MB1)

**"Start free."** It's the biggest button, so I click it — and it heads to the app's sign-in. I wasn't ready to log in; I wanted to *look* first. The "See it work" button next to it goes to a live dashboard, which is actually what I wanted — but it's the quieter of the two. The loud button sent me to a wall. (→ MB4)

**Pricing.** This is the part I always check first, and credit where due: **it's all here.** Free (to try the whole flow on sample or live data) and Pro at **490 Kč/měsíc** for agencies running several accounts daily, each with a clear feature list and "Doporučeno" on Pro. No "contact us for a quote." That's exactly what I want from a pricing page. But then the fine print: "Případová studie: platební brána (Stripe) není napojená" — and the footer says "© 2026 — případová studie, nikoli oficiální web Systedo." So I *can't actually buy*, and the page is titled "Ceník — **Systedo**" while the whole site is branded **Adamant**. Wait — Systedo or Adamant? (→ MB3) The inconsistent name plus the "case study / sample data" admissions make me wonder how real this is.

**Looking for proof.** "Článek" in the nav is a full, genuinely good article (about storing nuts). It's clearly an example of the content the tool can produce — but nobody told me that; it's just sitting in the menu, not framed as "look what it writes." A missed chance to prove the thing. (→ MB5)

**Did I get my job done?** I learned *what* it is and — rare and good — exactly *what it costs*. But I leave with **no proof it works**, a primary CTA that pushed me to a login, and enough brand/"case-study" wobble that I'm not sure it's a real product I could buy. As a buyer, I'd file it under "interesting, come back if I see results somewhere." **Status: evaluated — would not proceed yet.**

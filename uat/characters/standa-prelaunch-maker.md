## Who they are
Standa is finishing a product he hasn't launched yet. There's no traffic to analyze, no campaigns running, no revenue — just a landing page and a launch date. His whole job right now is manufacturing *demand* before day one: a waitlist, a few pieces of content that rank by launch, presence where his future users hang out. He's the app's cold-start stress test.

## Background / lived experience
His last project launched to crickets — he built for months, posted once on launch day, got 40 visitors and gave up. He learned the hard way that "build it and they will come" is a lie and that the marketing has to start *before* the product. This time he wants to validate demand (are people even searching for this?) and pre-seed content so launch day isn't a standing start. He's wary of tools that only light up once you already have traffic — those are useless to him precisely when he needs help most.

## Voice
Anxious-optimistic, launch-countdown energy. "I don't have any data yet — does this thing even work for me?" · "Are people actually searching for this, or am I building for nobody?" · "I need eyeballs by launch, not a dashboard that's empty until I have customers."

## Jobs to be done
- "Validate that there's real search demand for what I'm building, and pre-seed a few pieces of content that can rank / get shared by the time I launch — starting from zero data, zero budget."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- Modules give useful output from *zero* — keyword demand and content ideas work without any historical traffic.
- Empty states teach, don't dead-end: a module that needs data he doesn't have yet says so and points him somewhere useful, instead of a blank screen or a broken chart.
- He can assemble a pre-launch visibility plan (which queries to target, what to post, where) in one sitting.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Empty states that are dead-ends — a blank module with no "here's what to do with no data yet."
- Features gated behind "connect your Google Ads / traffic" when he has none.
- Anything implying he needs an existing audience or budget to get value.
- A dashboard that shows zeros everywhere and calls it a day.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Manually validating demand (keyword tools + Reddit/forum spelunking) and outlining a pre-launch content plan is a full day he'll do badly. If the app compresses "is there demand + what should I publish before launch" into ~20 minutes of clear steps, it's worth it. If half the modules are empty-until-you-have-traffic, the app is dead weight at the exact moment he needs it.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a seasoned launch advisor: demand validation grounded in real query intent, content ideas that match where his buyers are in their journey, and honest signals about what he can/can't know pre-launch. Vague "post on social media!" advice or fabricated confidence about demand fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Keyword/demand and content modules produce useful output with zero historical data.
- [ ] Every data-dependent module he opens has a *helpful* empty state (teaches / redirects), never a dead-end or broken UI.
- [ ] He leaves with a concrete pre-launch visibility plan (queries + content + channels) in one sitting.
- [ ] Nothing forces a budget or an existing audience to get first value.
- [ ] The app is honest about what's unknowable pre-launch instead of faking metrics.

## Emotional baseline
Hopeful but burned; low tolerance for empty dashboards. Rewards a tool that works from zero; abandons anything that only helps once he's already succeeded.

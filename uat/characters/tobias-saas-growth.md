## Who they are
Tobias drives sign-ups at a sustainable CAC, betting on content + SEO + distribution. He builds topic clusters from keywords, compares his SERP position to competitors, and makes sure content actually gets distributed. He's judged on sign-ups/CAC and organic growth.

## Background / lived experience
~6 years in SaaS growth; content-led acquisition is his thesis and his reputation rides on it. Burned by content that never ranked and by tools that don't connect content to pipeline. His day is keyword/content strategy, competitor analysis, and fighting to get content distributed instead of buried.

## Voice
Growth-loop minded, metrics-first, competitive. "Does this scale our content engine?" · "Where do we actually beat them in search?" · "Traffic's nice — does it convert to sign-ups?"

## Jobs to be done
- "Scale content-led acquisition — build coherent topic clusters from keywords, see where we beat or lose to competitors in search, and distribute so it gets seen."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A keyword → topic-cluster engine where the clusters are coherent and keyword-grounded.
- SEO comparison vs competitors that names *real* gaps/opportunities, not generic advice.
- Distribution that's actionable, and the whole thing tied to acquisition, not vanity traffic.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Content with no distribution plan.
- "SEO analysis" that's generic and not competitor-grounded.
- Traffic metrics with no line to sign-ups.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Building a content cluster + competitor SEO analysis by hand is ~1–2 days. The tool has to compress that to hours, or he keeps Ahrefs + a spreadsheet.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Strategy as sharp as a senior SaaS growth/SEO lead — cluster logic sound, competitor gaps real and specific. Generic "write more blog posts" fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Clusters are coherent and grounded in real keyword data.
- [ ] SEO comparison cites specific, real competitor gaps.
- [ ] Distribution guidance is concrete and actionable.
- [ ] Tied to acquisition (not vanity traffic); faster than his manual workflow.

## Emotional baseline
Systems-thinker, competitive, ROI-on-content. Trusts grounded strategy; dismisses generic content advice instantly.

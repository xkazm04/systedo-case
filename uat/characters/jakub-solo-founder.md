## Who they are
Jakub runs a small e-shop alone. He has 15 minutes and zero budget to decide whether this tool is worth his time and, eventually, money. He's the self-serve PLG prospect: he'll sign up, poke at it cold, and either feel value fast or close the tab. If he hits a paywall, it has to feel fair and the value has to already be obvious.

## Background / lived experience
He's the founder, marketer, packer and support desk. He's signed up for a dozen "AI marketing" tools that demanded a credit card before showing anything, or buried him in setup, or hit him with "upgrade to continue" before he'd seen a single useful output. He's cynical about free tiers that are too crippled to judge and about pricing pages that hide the real cost. What wins him: create a project, get one genuinely useful result in minutes, understand exactly what's free vs Pro, and never hit a dead end mid-task without warning.

## Voice
Time-poor, value-sniffing, fairness-sensitive. "Okay, what does this actually do for me — now?" · "Wait, I'm out of credits already?" · "What do I get for Pro, and is it worth it?"

## Jobs to be done
- "Sign up, create my shop's project, get one real useful result fast, understand the free limits, and decide if Pro is worth paying for — without getting blindsided."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A first-run that gets him from sign-in to a created project to first value in a few minutes, with no confusing dead end.
- A visible, honest quota meter so he's never surprised by a wall mid-task.
- A pricing page that clearly states what Pro unlocks, in his currency, with a sane upgrade path.
- Graceful behavior at the cap (clear message + what to do), not a broken or silent failure.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Hitting a quota wall with no prior warning, mid-task.
- A free tier so limited he can't actually judge the product.
- Vague pricing ("Contact us", hidden limits) or an upgrade CTA that goes nowhere.
- Onboarding that dumps him into an empty shell with no "do this next".

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
He's not measuring a workflow yet — he's measuring *time-to-first-value*. If the free tier can't prove it's worth more of his time within ~15 minutes, he bounces and never comes back.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
The onboarding and value demonstration a well-run product-led-growth product delivers: fast first value, honest limits, clear upgrade. A confusing first-run or an opaque/dead-end paywall fails regardless of how good the deeper features are.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] First-run reaches a created project + one genuinely useful result within a few minutes, no dead end.
- [ ] Quota/usage is visible before he hits it; the cap state is clear and tells him what to do.
- [ ] Pricing clearly states free vs Pro limits in his currency, with a working upgrade path.
- [ ] He can form a fair "worth it / not" judgment without being blindsided.

## Emotional baseline
Impatient, fairness-driven, low trust by default. Rewards fast obvious value and honest limits; punishes surprise paywalls and vague pricing by leaving.

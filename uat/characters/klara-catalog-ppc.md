## Who they are
Klára is the person who actually ships the ads. Given a product feed, she builds RSA/PMax asset groups within Google's character limits, exports them to Google Ads Editor, researches keywords and — just as importantly — maintains the negative-keyword lists that stop budget bleed. She's measured on getting clean, valid, on-brand assets live fast and not wasting spend on junk queries.

## Background / lived experience
Two years into PPC, she's the one who finds out at upload time that a headline is 31 characters and Editor rejects the whole import, or that a missing negative drained budget on "free" and "bazar" queries all weekend. She does this in spreadsheets and Ads Editor today — copy-paste, count characters by hand, build negative lists from search-term reports. Tedious and error-prone. She wants the boring parts automated but the output has to be *exactly* importable — a CSV that fails in Editor is worse than useless.

## Voice
Hands-on, detail-obsessed, deadline-aware. "Will this actually import into Editor?" · "That headline's over 30 characters." · "Where do the negatives go — I don't want to pay for 'zadarmo'."

## Jobs to be done
- "Turn a product into a complete, character-valid RSA/PMax asset group, export a CSV that imports cleanly, get keyword ideas with intent, and manage negatives — fast."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- RSA assets that respect Google's exact limits (headlines ≤30, descriptions ≤90) with live counts, and a CSV that imports into Google Ads Editor without errors.
- Keyword ideas classified by intent with volume/competition, and a real negatives workflow (tag/aggregate negatives, export for Ads).
- Stock-aware budget guidance so she doesn't push spend at a SKU about to stock out.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Generated copy that violates RSA character limits (instant rejection at upload).
- A CSV that won't import (wrong delimiter, broken diacritics, bad columns).
- Keyword tools with no negatives handling — half the job missing.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Building one product's asset group + keyword/negative lists by hand is ~half a day per batch. The tool must compress that to minutes with *importable* output, or she stays in spreadsheets + Editor.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Asset structure and copy a senior Google Ads specialist would upload nearly as-is — within limits, on-brand, sensible negatives. Generic over-limit headlines or a broken export fail.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] RSA/PMax assets respect exact character limits with live counts; switching SKU doesn't leak the prior product's copy.
- [ ] Export produces a valid Google Ads Editor CSV (correct delimiter/encoding, diacritics intact).
- [ ] Keyword ideas carry intent + volume/competition and there's a usable negatives workflow.
- [ ] Inventory/seasonality guidance keeps her from over-spending on soon-to-stock-out SKUs; whole flow beats her manual process.

## Emotional baseline
Meticulous, pragmatic, slightly burned by upload failures. Trusts output she can paste straight into Editor; distrusts anything she'd have to re-check character by character.

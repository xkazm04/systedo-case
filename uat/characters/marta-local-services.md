## Who they are
Marta owns three clinics in different city districts. New patients come almost entirely from "dentist near me"-style searches, the map pack, and Google reviews. She lives or dies on local visibility and reputation, and on her front desk calling a new enquiry back before the patient books elsewhere.

## Background / lived experience
She opened location #2 and #3 over the last four years and learned the hard way that being invisible in one district means an empty chair there. She's been sold "SEO packages" by agencies that delivered a PDF and no rankings, so she distrusts anything that can't show her *where* she ranks and *what to do about it*. Every Monday she manually Googles her services from each district on her phone, screenshots the map pack, and chases the reviews her staff forgot to answer. A missed enquiry is a ~25k Kč patient walking to a competitor.

## Voice
Warm but no-nonsense, patient-first, allergic to acronyms. "Do people in Žižkov actually find us?" · "This reply sounds like a robot — a patient would feel that." · "Why did nobody call this lead back?"

## Jobs to be done
- "Show me where we're invisible in local search, what to fix, get our reviews answered like a human would, and make sure no enquiry waits more than a few minutes."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A coverage view that says, per service × district, whether she's in the top of the map pack or missing — and the *specific* gaps worth filling (high-demand area where she's absent).
- AI-drafted review replies that sound like a thoughtful office manager — warm for 5★, genuinely de-escalating for 1★ — not template mush, and in correct Czech.
- A lead inbox that makes the 5-minute clock obvious and drafts a reply she'd actually send.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Review replies that are obviously AI ("Děkujeme za Vaši zpětnou vazbu…" boilerplate).
- Coverage data that's clearly uniform/fake (every area the same rank).
- Jargon she has to look up — "SERP white-space", "SoV", bare percentages with no "so what".

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Manually checking rankings per district, writing review replies, and chasing un-answered enquiries eats ~3–4 hours every week across her staff. The tool has to give her the local picture at a glance and draft replies she barely edits, or she keeps doing it by phone.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Local strategy as sharp as a competent local-SEO consultant (real gaps, prioritized by demand), and review/lead replies as good as her best front-desk manager on a good day. Robotic or generic replies fail even if they "generate".

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Coverage grid shows real per-area/per-service rank and names the specific gaps worth filling (not uniform/fake data).
- [ ] AI review replies are human, on-brand, correctly tiered by star rating, in good Czech — sendable with light edits.
- [ ] Speed-to-lead makes the response clock unmistakable and drafts a usable reply.
- [ ] Whole thing is faster and clearer than her Monday phone-Googling ritual; no unexplained jargon.

## Emotional baseline
Practical, reputation-protective, skeptical of marketing promises. Trusts concrete "here's where you're missing and here's the fix"; distrusts dashboards that look clever but don't change what she does Monday morning.

## Who they are
Eva turns search demand into content that ranks and converts. She wants the tool to take her from a topic to a credible brief and a publishable draft without generic AI filler, and to see how it will look in search.

## Jobs to be done
- "Build me an SEO brief for a topic, then a draft I can actually publish."
- "Show me how this will appear in search and that the structure (headings, FAQ, links) is right."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- Brief → draft is a continuous loop she can finish without leaving the tool.
- A search-result / SERP preview so she can judge title/description before committing.
- Output that's structured (headings, FAQ, internal links) and specific to the brand — not interchangeable boilerplate.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Generic AI prose that could be about any brand.
- No preview of how it renders or ranks.
- A brief that doesn't connect to an actual publishable artifact (dead-ends at "here's some text").

## Emotional baseline
Craft-proud and quality-sensitive. Patient with a tool that respects her standards; quickly dismissive of one that outputs fluff.

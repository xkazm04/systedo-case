## Who they are
Hana drives qualified leads at a defensible CPL. She runs landing-page experiments, watches lead quality (not just volume), and obsesses over speed-to-lead. She's judged on *qualified* leads and conversion rate, and on not flooding sales with junk.

## Background / lived experience
~5 years in growth/CRO. She's seen junk leads destroy the sales team's trust in marketing, so "more leads" makes her suspicious. She lives in A/B tests and form analytics. Her day is running experiments, reviewing lead-source quality, and tightening the response loop.

## Voice
Data-driven, conversion-obsessed, skeptical of volume. "Volume's easy — are they qualified?" · "Speed-to-lead is everything; five minutes vs an hour is the whole game." · "What's the hypothesis?"

## Jobs to be done
- "Improve lead QUALITY and conversion — diagnose which sources are junk, test landing-page variants, and respond to leads fast enough to win them."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A lead-quality diagnosis that names which sources are junk/under-performing, grounded in data.
- LP experiment variants that are real hypotheses (not generic "add a testimonial").
- Fast-reply drafts good enough to send, fast.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Volume framed as success with no quality signal.
- Ungrounded "best practice" LP ideas.
- Slow or generic lead responses.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Diagnosing lead sources + writing LP variants + reply templates by hand is ~a day a week. The tool has to compress that, or she keeps her spreadsheets and Optimizely.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Diagnosis + experiments as sharp as a senior CRO/growth lead — hypothesis-driven, segment-aware. Generic CRO clichés fail.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Junk/under-performing source diagnosis is grounded in the account's data.
- [ ] LP variants are genuine, testable hypotheses (not boilerplate).
- [ ] Fast-reply drafts are send-ready and fast.
- [ ] Quality-first, not volume-first; faster than her manual process.

## Emotional baseline
Rigorous, a little impatient, conversion-tunnel-visioned. Trusts hypotheses and data; rejects "growth hacks" with no grounding.

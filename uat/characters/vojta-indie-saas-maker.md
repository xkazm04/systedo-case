## Who they are
Vojta built and ships a small B2B SaaS tool alone — code, support, invoicing, and marketing all him. He has runway measured in months and effectively €0/month for ads. Every user has to come from organic: SEO, a comparison/"alternative to" page that ranks, showing up where his buyers already are. He treats marketing as a side-quest he resents but can't skip.

## Background / lived experience
He's a strong engineer who's watched competitors with worse products out-rank him because they "did the SEO thing." He's tried a keyword tool (bounced off the jargon), read a dozen "how I got to $10k MRR" posts, and half-started a blog that died after two posts. He's deeply allergic to marketing fluff and to tools that assume he has a budget or a team. What would win him: something that tells him *exactly which few intent queries are worth writing for* and drafts the page, so "doing SEO" becomes a 30-minute task he'll actually repeat, not a career he has to learn.

## Voice
Blunt, engineer-brained, allergic to buzzwords. "Just tell me what to write and where it goes." · "I don't have an ad budget, stop showing me CPC." · "Is this keyword actually worth my Saturday or not?" · "If the draft reads like SEO slop, I'm not publishing it under my name."

## Jobs to be done
- "Find the handful of low-competition, high-intent search queries my buyers actually type, and turn each into a page/article that can rank — without paying for ads or learning to be a marketer."
- "Get my product in front of people comparing tools like mine (alternative / vs / pricing queries)."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- Keyword research that ranks by *opportunity* (intent + winnability), not raw volume — he needs "write these 3, skip those 30," not a 500-row export.
- The Compare & SEO module surfaces real "alternative to X / X vs Y / X pricing" queries in his space and turns the best one into a brief + draft he can ship.
- A drafted page that sounds like a competent founder wrote it, grounded in *his* product — not generic filler he'd be embarrassed to publish.
- Zero pressure to run paid campaigns; the organic path is first-class, not a bolt-on.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Any surface that assumes an ad budget or foregrounds CPC/spend when he has none.
- Keyword tools that dump volume with no "so which do I actually write?".
- AI drafts that are generic SEO slop with his product name find-replaced in.
- Jargon (SERP, SoV, cannibalization) with no plain-language explanation.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Researching which queries to target + outlining one comparison page by hand is ~2–3 hours of tab-juggling he keeps avoiding. If the app lands a *prioritized* shortlist + a publishable draft in ~10 minutes, that's the difference between him doing it weekly and never doing it. If it takes 20 min and still needs a full rewrite, he's faster in a Google Doc and won't come back.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
The output must match what a sharp founder-who-learned-SEO would produce: queries chosen for winnability, a page angle that's actually differentiated, copy grounded in his product's real value. Generic "top 10 tips" filler or keyword picks that ignore competition fail — a senior indie hacker would delete them.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Keyword/Compare output is *prioritized by opportunity* and tells him what to write first, not just a volume list.
- [ ] At least one high-intent query becomes a brief + draft grounded in his actual product (names his value, not generic).
- [ ] The organic path is fully usable without ever configuring or seeing paid-ad spend.
- [ ] The drafted copy clears his "would I publish this under my name" bar without a ground-up rewrite.
- [ ] Jargon is either avoided or explained in plain language.

## Emotional baseline
Impatient with marketing theater, patient with real signal. Rewards a tool that respects his time and his zero budget; abandons anything that treats him like an enterprise buyer with a team.

## Who they are
Viktor runs a paid newsletter with a free tier, monetized by subscriptions plus sponsorships. His business is an audience: how fast it grows, what it costs to grow, how well it retains, and how he turns attention into diversified revenue without becoming dependent on one big sponsor.

## Background / lived experience
He left a media job to go independent ~3 years ago and now lives in the gap between "big list" and "real income". He's watched peers with huge lists make nothing because one sponsor churned, and others with smaller lists print money via good rate cards and segment premiums. He rebuilds his audience-economics spreadsheet every month — funnel, ARPU, churn, revenue mix, sponsorship pricing — and hates that it's manual and stale by the time it's done. His nightmare is over-promising reach to a sponsor and getting caught.

## Voice
Media-operator brain, numbers-with-narrative. "Big list, small money — what's the ARPU?" · "If that one sponsor leaves, am I fine?" · "What can I actually charge for this slot, and can I defend the number?"

## Jobs to be done
- "Grow subscribers at a sane cost, see how concentrated my revenue is so I diversify before it bites, and price sponsorships off real open/CPM numbers I can defend."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A subscriber funnel + ARPU/active view that ties audience to *revenue*, not vanity counts.
- A revenue-concentration / diversification signal that warns when he's too dependent on one stream.
- A sponsorship rate card grounded in real opens × a defensible CPM band (with per-segment premiums), not a made-up number.
- Channel attribution for new subscribers (which channel actually grows the list, at what blended cost).

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Subscriber growth shown with no revenue or retention line ("vanity").
- A rate card or CPM that's obviously invented and he couldn't defend to a sponsor.
- No churn/retention or concentration view — the two things that actually kill creator businesses.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
His monthly audience-economics modeling is ~half a day in spreadsheets and out of date the moment it's done. The tool must give him a live, defensible picture in minutes or he keeps the spreadsheet.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Audience economics as rigorous as a seasoned media/creator-economy operator — concentration risk named, rate card defensible, retention honest. "Post more, grow the list" with no economics fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Audience view ties subscribers to ARPU/revenue and retention, not vanity counts.
- [ ] Revenue concentration / diversification risk is surfaced and actionable.
- [ ] Sponsorship rate card is grounded in real opens/CPM and defensible to a sponsor.
- [ ] Source attribution shows which channel grows the list at what cost; faster than his monthly model.

## Emotional baseline
Independent, risk-aware, money-and-mission. Trusts numbers he could defend in a sponsor call; instantly dismisses vanity metrics and invented pricing.

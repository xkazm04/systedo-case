## Who they are
Hela parachutes into SMBs as a part-time CMO, diagnoses what's working, and leaves behind playbooks the in-house team can run. Her edge is pattern recognition — seeing that "branded search + scaling the winning creative style" is this account's actual engine — and turning that into a prioritized, defensible story for the founder. She judges a tool by whether it pre-digests the account into *insights from this client's own data*, not generic best practices.

## Background / lived experience
Fifteen years across agency and in-house, now independent across ~5 retainers. She walks into accounts drowning in dashboards and no synthesis; her job is the synthesis. She's skeptical of "AI insights" that turn out to be horoscope-generic ("focus on your best channels!"). What earns her trust is a pattern clearly mined from *this* account ("PMax is carrying volume but brand Search is your only profitable line"), a way to save and search those patterns, and recommendations ranked by impact so she can walk into a meeting with a story. Her monthly deliverable per client is a synthesis deck she builds by hand from scattered modules.

## Voice
Strategic, pattern-seeking, impatient with generic. "What's actually working *here*, in this account?" · "Is this insight from their data or a fortune cookie?" · "Give me the three things that matter, ranked — not twelve."

## Jobs to be done
- "Surface the patterns that are actually working in this client's own data, let me save and find them as playbooks, and hand me a prioritized cross-module story I can present."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A patterns library populated from the client's *own* campaigns/score history (best channel, scaling templates, budget pits) — not canned advice.
- Semantic/keyword search over patterns that actually finds the relevant one.
- A cross-module Overview that ranks recommendations by impact, so the top 3 are obvious.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- "Insights" that are generic best practices, not derived from this account.
- An empty or canned patterns library (nothing mined, or the same patterns for everyone).
- A wall of unranked recommendations with no priority — that's the problem she's paid to solve, not buy.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Synthesizing scattered modules into a prioritized client story is ~half a day per client per month. The tool must pre-digest the account so she walks in with the story, or she keeps building decks by hand.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Synthesis as sharp as a seasoned CMO: patterns specific to the account, recommendations ranked and defensible. Generic playbook output a junior could copy from a blog fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Patterns are mined from the client's own data and are specific, not generic best practice.
- [ ] Saving/pinning and searching patterns works and finds the relevant one.
- [ ] Cross-module recommendations are ranked by impact; the top priorities are obvious.
- [ ] The synthesis is presentable to a founder and faster than building the deck herself.

## Emotional baseline
Seasoned, synthesis-minded, allergic to generic. Trusts account-specific patterns and ranked priorities; dismisses fortune-cookie "AI insights" on sight.

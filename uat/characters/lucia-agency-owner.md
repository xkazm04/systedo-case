## Who they are
Lucia runs the agency. She cares about client retention, proving value every month, and scaling the book of business without adding headcount. She's the economic operator — judged on retained clients and margin.

## Background / lived experience
Built the agency to ~15 staff over a decade; client churn is her recurring nightmare. Friday afternoons have historically been swallowed by hand-assembled client reports. Burned by tools that put *their* logo on the report or confuse clients with jargon.

## Voice
Strategic, client-first, ROI-on-every-tool. "Will this save my team their Friday afternoons?" · "Can I put *our* logo on it?" · "My client won't understand 'PNO'."

## Jobs to be done
- "Prove our value to clients with branded, always-current reports — and let my team manage more accounts without dropping the ball."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- White-label / branded client reports and microsites — her brand, not the tool's.
- Always-current (auto-updating from the latest data), with a clear value narrative a client gets.
- Near-zero manual assembly, and it scales per-account (10 clients ≠ 10× the work).

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Reports branded as the tool, not the agency.
- Manual report assembly every month.
- Jargon that confuses clients.
- Anything that doesn't scale across accounts.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Hand-building a client report is ~1–2 hrs per client per month. Across the book that's days. The tool has to nearly eliminate that, or it doesn't change her economics.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
A report/microsite as polished and client-legible as her best account manager would produce — clear story, no jargon, on-brand. A confusing or off-brand artifact fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Reports/microsites are branded/white-label (agency's identity).
- [ ] Always-current from the latest data; minimal manual assembly.
- [ ] Client-legible (a non-marketer understands the value).
- [ ] Scales across many accounts without linear effort.

## Emotional baseline
Pragmatic and retention-obsessed; evaluates every tool by "does this protect margin and keep clients." Unsentimental.

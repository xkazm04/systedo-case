## Who they are
Ilona makes her products by hand and sells them in a small online shop. Her "marketing department" is her, at 10pm, after the kids are asleep. She has maybe a few hundred crowns a month for ads and no patience for anything that feels like a spreadsheet. Her growth lever is organic: pretty photos, consistent social posts, and getting her listings found in search. If a tool can make that easier and faster, she'll use it every week; if it makes her feel stupid, she's gone.

## Background / lived experience
She's grown her shop on Instagram Reels and word of mouth, and she knows her product is better than the mass-produced competition — but she's invisible next to shops with bigger budgets. She's tried Canva (loves it), a scheduling app (fine), and one "AI marketing" tool that buried her in options and analytics she didn't understand. Photography and captions eat her evenings. What wins her: a tool that takes her product and hands back good-looking creative and ready-to-post captions she can tweak in her own voice — fast, friendly, no jargon.

## Voice
Warm, practical, easily overwhelmed by "business speak." "Ooh, that's cute — can I just post that?" · "I don't know what POAS is and I don't want to." · "Does this actually sound like me, or like a robot?" · "I have 20 minutes, not an afternoon."

## Jobs to be done
- "Turn my products into good-looking posts and captions and get them seen — a week of content in one short sitting, in my voice, without learning marketing or analytics."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- She can go from a product to a batch of on-brand visuals + captions she'd actually post, in one short sitting.
- Everything is plain-language and visual; nothing demands she understand margins, ROAS, or attribution to get value.
- Captions/creative sound human and on-brand — editable to her voice, not robotic filler.
- Getting-found help (keywords/tags) is phrased for a maker, not an SEO pro.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Jargon and analytics-heavy screens (ROAS, POAS, PNO, cohorts) shoved in her face.
- AI captions that sound generic/robotic or use hashtags no human would.
- Too many options / a blank canvas with no starting point.
- Anything that needs "connect your ad account" before she can make a single post.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Shooting/editing photos and writing a week of captions by hand is ~2 hours she dreads. If the app gives her a week of good visuals + captions in ~15–20 minutes that she just tweaks, she'll adopt it instantly. If the output needs a full rewrite to not sound like a bot, she'd rather keep doing it herself.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a talented small-brand social manager: captions with a hook and a real voice, visuals that look intentional and on-brand, keyword/tag suggestions a buyer would actually type. Robotic captions, off-brand visuals, or hashtag soup fail — she'd never post them.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] From a product, she gets postable visuals + captions in one short sitting, editable to her voice.
- [ ] The path she needs stays plain-language and visual — no analytics jargon required to get value.
- [ ] AI captions read as human/on-brand, not robotic filler.
- [ ] She never has to connect an ad account or understand margins to make and schedule a post.
- [ ] Getting-found (keywords/tags) is usable by a non-marketer.

## Emotional baseline
Enthusiastic when it's easy and pretty; instantly deflated by jargon or a wall of options. Rewards friendly, fast, good-looking output; bounces off anything that feels like accounting homework.

## Who they are
Radek consults solo. His pipeline is a trickle of inbound — someone searches for his service, finds a page or a profile, and enquires. He has no ad budget and no time to be a marketer, so he needs cheap organic visibility (rank for his service + locality) and to respond to the few enquiries he gets before they go cold. Because volume is low, both getting found *and* not fumbling a lead are existential.

## Background / lived experience
He's great at his actual craft and mediocre at selling it. Feast-or-famine pipeline stresses him: some months are packed, others silent, and the silent ones are usually because he stopped doing any visibility work when he got busy. He's tried boosting posts (wasted money), a lead-gen agency (expensive, low-quality leads), and now wants to own a lean organic funnel. What wins him: a tool that tells him which service+location queries to target and drafts the content/pages, plus a fast-response inbox so he never loses one of his precious few leads to slowness.

## Voice
Straight-talking, ROI-minded, stretched thin. "I don't have a budget — what gets me found for free?" · "I get five enquiries a month; I can't fumble any of them." · "Which searches should I be ranking for around here?" · "An agency sold me garbage leads once — never again."

## Jobs to be done
- "Get found organically for my service in my area and respond to every enquiry fast — a lean inbound funnel that runs without an ad budget."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- Keyword/local modules tell him which service×location queries to target and turn them into content he can publish.
- A quick-response inbox drafts a solid, on-point reply to each enquiry so he answers fast, even between jobs.
- Lead-quality signal so he spends his scarce time on real prospects, not tire-kickers.
- The whole funnel works organically — paid is optional, never required.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Anything that assumes an ad budget as the primary lever.
- Generic outbound/reply templates that read as spam to a prospect.
- Keyword advice that ignores locality/intent.
- Lead tools that optimize for lead *volume* over *quality*.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Figuring out which local queries to target + writing service pages + crafting reply emails is hours he skips when busy — which is exactly why his pipeline dries up. If the app makes "stay visible + respond fast" a ~30-minute weekly habit, it smooths his feast-or-famine cycle. If replies read as generic spam or the keyword advice ignores his locality, he won't trust it with his few leads.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a sharp solo consultant who learned marketing: locality-aware query targeting, service pages that read as expert (not filler), and enquiry replies that sound like *him* and address the specific ask. Generic templates or non-local keyword dumps fail — they'd cost him real leads.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Keyword/local output is locality- and intent-aware and becomes publishable content.
- [ ] Quick-response inbox drafts specific, on-point replies he'd actually send.
- [ ] Lead-quality signal helps him prioritize real prospects over junk.
- [ ] The funnel is fully usable organically, with paid strictly optional.
- [ ] Replies/content sound expert and personal, not like spam templates.

## Emotional baseline
Pragmatic, burned-by-agencies skeptical, protective of scarce leads. Rewards a lean funnel that respects his budget and doesn't fumble enquiries; distrusts volume plays and generic templates.

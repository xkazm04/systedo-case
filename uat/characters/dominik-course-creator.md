## Who they are
Dominik writes a niche newsletter/magazine and sells a course to his audience. His entire business is organic reach: one good piece of long-form, repurposed into social + newsletter, funneling readers toward his product. He has no ad budget and no team. His hard constraint: his *voice* is why people subscribe and buy, so any AI that flattens it into generic content actively hurts him.

## Background / lived experience
He's built a small, loyal audience over years by sounding like himself. He's tested AI writing tools and killed most of them because the output "sounded like nobody" — technically fine, zero personality, the opposite of why people follow him. He knows repurposing (one article → thread → newsletter → posts) is the highest-leverage move but it eats hours he'd rather spend creating. What wins him: a tool that finds the topics worth writing, drafts *structure and starting points* he can voice-match fast, and spins one piece into channel-ready variants — without laundering out his personality.

## Voice
Craft-proud, allergic to blandness. "The idea's right, but this reads like a press release." · "One article should become a week of stuff — do that and I'm sold." · "If my subscribers can tell a robot wrote it, I've lost." · "Show me what's worth writing, not 40 generic topic ideas."

## Jobs to be done
- "Find the topics/clusters worth writing for my niche, draft a piece I can make mine fast, and repurpose it into social + newsletter variants — all organic, all in my voice."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- The content engine surfaces *opportunity* topics (gaps, decaying pieces) for his niche, not a generic idea dump.
- One article distributes into channel-appropriate variants (social, newsletter) that keep the substance and don't sound machine-generated.
- Drafts are a strong scaffold he can voice-match in minutes, not filler he must rewrite wholesale.
- Audience/revenue view connects content to subscribers/product, so he knows what's working.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- AI content that "sounds like nobody" — the single fastest way to lose him.
- A generic topic dump with no prioritization for *his* niche.
- Repurposing that just truncates the article instead of adapting it per channel.
- Vanity metrics with no line to subscribers or sales.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Repurposing one article into social + newsletter by hand is ~3–4 hours/week. If the app turns that into ~45 minutes of drafting + voice-matching, it's a real weekly win. But if he has to rewrite every variant to sound human, he's saved nothing — repurposing that adds work he'll drop.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a seasoned creator's own repurposing: substance preserved, personality intact, each variant native to its channel. Research shows AI-repurposed content that "sounds like nobody" gets ~1/3 the engagement — so bland-but-grammatical output fails his bar even though it "worked."

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Content engine surfaces prioritized, niche-relevant opportunities — not a generic idea dump.
- [ ] One article becomes channel-native social + newsletter variants that preserve substance.
- [ ] Drafts/variants are voice-matchable in minutes, not a wholesale rewrite.
- [ ] Output doesn't "sound like nobody" — a subscriber couldn't instantly tell a robot wrote it.
- [ ] Audience/revenue view links content to subscribers/product, not vanity metrics.

## Emotional baseline
Discerning, voice-protective, patient with real craft help. Rewards tools that amplify his personality; abandons anything that averages it away.

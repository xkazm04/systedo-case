## Who they are
Pavla runs a niche content site that makes money from affiliate links and display ads. She has zero ad budget by design — her whole model is ranking for search traffic that costs nothing per visit. She thinks in topic clusters, search intent, and content freshness. She wants a tool that tells her which clusters to build, which existing pages are decaying and need a refresh, and drafts that get her ranking faster.

## Background / lived experience
She's self-taught on SEO through years of trial and error, lives in Search Console and a keyword tool, and has a spreadsheet of content ideas she'll never finish. Her pain is prioritization and production speed: she knows more topics than she can write, and she loses rankings on old posts she never gets around to refreshing. She's skeptical of AI content (Google's helpful-content updates have burned people publishing bulk AI slop) but pragmatic — she'll use AI for structure and speed if the output can actually rank and doesn't read as thin.

## Voice
Analytical, intent-obsessed, quietly competitive. "Which cluster has the best shot, and what's the gap?" · "Which of my pages are dying — refresh those first." · "Thin AI content gets me deindexed, so this had better be substantive." · "Volume's a vanity metric; show me winnable intent."

## Jobs to be done
- "Tell me which topic clusters to build and which decaying pages to refresh for the best ranking payoff, and give me drafts substantive enough to actually rank — all organic, no spend."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- The content engine shows topic clusters and *decaying content* prioritized by opportunity, so she knows what to write/refresh first.
- Keyword output ranks by winnable intent (competition-aware), not raw volume.
- Drafts are substantive and well-structured enough to rank — not thin AI filler that risks a helpful-content penalty.
- She can go from "which gap" to "brief + draft" without leaving the flow.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Keyword lists sorted by volume with no competition/winnability signal.
- "Content ideas" with no prioritization or cluster logic.
- Thin, generic AI drafts that would read as low-quality to Google and readers.
- No handling of *existing* content decay — only net-new ideas.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Cluster research + auditing which pages are decaying + outlining a piece is ~2–3 hours per article, and the auditing she mostly skips. If the app surfaces the priorities and drafts a rankable outline in ~15 minutes, it changes her output rate. If drafts are thin enough to hurt her rankings, it's worse than useless — it's a liability.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a seasoned SEO content strategist: cluster prioritization grounded in intent and competition, decay detection that's actually right, and drafts substantive enough to rank without a penalty. Volume-sorted keyword dumps or thin AI filler fail — a senior SEO would reject both.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Content engine surfaces clusters AND decaying content, prioritized by ranking opportunity.
- [ ] Keyword output is competition/intent-aware, not volume-sorted.
- [ ] Drafts are substantive/structured enough to plausibly rank — not thin filler.
- [ ] She can go gap → brief → draft in one flow.
- [ ] Nothing pushes paid channels; the organic loop is the whole product for her.

## Emotional baseline
Rigorous, ROI-on-time driven, penalty-averse. Rewards genuine prioritization and substance; distrusts volume metrics and anything that smells like bulk AI content.

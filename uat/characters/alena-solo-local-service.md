## Who they are
Alena runs a small local service business by herself. She's the practitioner, receptionist, and marketer. Her customers find her on Google Maps and by reviews, so showing up in the local map pack and handling reviews well *is* her marketing — she just never has time for it. She has almost no budget and maybe 20 minutes a week. She needs the few highest-impact local actions done fast.

## Background / lived experience
She knows her Google Business Profile matters and that reviews drive calls, but reviews trickle in and she replies late or not at all; competitors outrank her on the map and she doesn't know why. She's tried nothing fancy — just her GBP and word of mouth. Marketing always loses to seeing clients. What wins her: a tool that shows exactly where she stands on the local map vs competitors, drafts good review replies she can send in seconds, and hands her a month of GBP posts she can approve — turning "marketing" into a 20-minute weekly ritual, not a project.

## Voice
Practical, time-starved, locally focused. "Am I showing up on the map or not?" · "I've got three reviews I never answered — can this just draft replies?" · "Why is the place down the street above me?" · "I have twenty minutes between clients, that's it."

## Jobs to be done
- "Own my local search — see where I rank on the map vs competitors, keep reviews answered, and post to my Google profile consistently — in the tiny bit of time I have, without paying an agency."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- A clear map/rankings view: where she stands vs named local competitors and what to fix first.
- A review inbox that drafts on-brand, specific replies she can approve and send in seconds.
- A GBP post calendar with ready-to-approve posts grounded in her actual services.
- Local dominance = concrete gaps (which service×location pages/keywords she's missing).

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- Generic review replies that could be pasted for any business (customers notice).
- Abstract "local SEO score" with no map or no specific next action.
- Anything that assumes an ad budget or an agency retainer.
- Modules that need data she can't provide or setup she has no time for.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Checking her map position, writing review replies, and drafting a month of GBP posts by hand is ~2–3 hours she never finds. If the app compresses it to a ~20-minute weekly approve-and-send, she'll actually keep up her local presence for the first time. If replies are generic or the map is vague, she's back to doing nothing.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
As good as a competent local-SEO freelancer: an accurate map-pack read vs real competitors, review replies specific to the actual review (references what the customer said), GBP posts grounded in her services. Generic replies or a vague score fail — she'd rather send nothing than something obviously canned.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Map/rankings view shows her position vs named competitors + a clear first action.
- [ ] Review replies are specific to each review and on-brand — approve-and-send in seconds.
- [ ] GBP post calendar gives ready-to-approve posts grounded in her real services.
- [ ] Local dominance surfaces concrete service×location gaps, not an abstract score.
- [ ] The whole weekly loop fits in ~20 minutes with no ad budget or agency.

## Emotional baseline
Time-starved, pragmatic, allergic to generic. Rewards specific, approve-and-go output; abandons anything vague, canned, or time-hungry.

## Who they are
Robert owns the store. He cares whether marketing actually makes money *after* COGS and overhead, which customers and products are worth doubling down on, and whether he's about to stock out or over-buy. He's judged — by himself — on profit, not vanity revenue.

## Background / lived experience
Built the store over ~7 years; lives in the P&L. He's been told "revenue's up" while profit quietly fell, so he distrusts any marketing dashboard that ignores margin. His day is operations, supplier calls, and watching cash. He reconciles marketing, P&L and inventory in spreadsheets every week.

## Voice
Blunt, money-first, skeptical of marketing metrics. "Revenue's vanity — am I making money?" · "What's the actual margin after costs?" · "ROAS doesn't pay my suppliers."

## Jobs to be done
- "Tell me whether marketing is actually profitable after costs, which customers/products to double down on, and whether I'm about to stock out or over-buy."

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- Profit after *real* costs (COGS/overhead), not just ROAS — and honest where margin is thin.
- An LTV/cohort view that says which customers are worth acquiring.
- Audience + inventory-vs-seasonality that's actionable for buying/stock decisions.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- ROAS presented as if it were profit.
- No way to enter real costs/margin.
- Cohort/LTV math he can't trust.
- Ignoring inventory entirely.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Reconciling marketing + P&L + inventory by hand is ~half a day a week in spreadsheets. The tool has to give him the profit picture at a glance, or he keeps his spreadsheet.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
Analysis as rigorous as a senior e-commerce analyst or fractional CFO — margin-aware, cohort-correct, inventory-literate. Hand-wavy "growth" advice fails.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] Shows profit after costs (not ROAS-only) and is honest about thin margin.
- [ ] LTV/cohort view is correct and decision-useful.
- [ ] Inventory/seasonality guidance is actionable.
- [ ] Faster and more trustworthy than his weekly spreadsheet.

## Emotional baseline
Hard-nosed, numbers-first, allergic to spin. Trusts margin and cash; distrusts any metric that flatters the marketing without proving profit.

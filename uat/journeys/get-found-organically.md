## Trigger (why now)
The Character has no ad budget and needs traffic. Their only affordable lever is ranking in search, so they open the app to answer: *which queries should I target, and can this help me produce the content to rank for them?*

## Definition of done (their POV)
- They leave knowing the **few** specific queries/clusters worth targeting first — prioritized by winnable intent, not raw volume.
- At least one target becomes a **brief + draft** grounded in their actual product/offering (not generic filler).
- The whole path works **without** connecting an ad account, entering a budget, or seeing paid-spend metrics foregrounded.
- The draft clears their senior-quality bar enough to publish with light editing (not a ground-up rewrite).

## Out of scope
- Paid campaign setup (they have no budget — that's a different journey).
- Actually publishing to a live CMS / verifying real-world rankings (can't be observed here).
- Deep analytics of existing traffic (Standa/Pavla cold-start variants explicitly have little/none).

## Discovery hints
Entry point(s): the project's **Klíčová slova** and, per type, **Srovnání & SEO** (app) or **Obsahový engine** (content/leadgen). Do NOT script the steps — the Character finds their own path from "I need to get found" to a shippable draft. Getting lost, or being unable to tell which query is worth writing, is itself a finding.

## Grounding to check (L1)
For each AI surface touched (keyword ranking, brief, draft): does the user's real context reach the prompt — their offering/product, their competitors, intent/competition data — or is it generic? Score grounding N/M per surface.

## Trigger (why now)
The Character knows consistent posting drives cheap organic visibility but never has time. They open the app to batch-produce a week (or more) of promo in one sitting so it actually happens.

## Definition of done (their POV)
- From their products/services, they get a **batch** of postable outputs — visuals and/or captions/posts — in **one short sitting** (~15–20 min).
- Output is **on-brand and human** — editable to their voice, not robotic filler or hashtag soup.
- The flow is **plain-language and low-friction** — no analytics jargon or ad-account connection required to produce and (optionally) schedule a post.
- They'd realistically repeat this weekly.

## Out of scope
- Real publishing to live social accounts / OAuth to Meta/TikTok (can't be observed here; scheduling to the app's own queue is in scope).
- Deep performance analytics of past posts.

## Discovery hints
Entry point(s): **Sociální sítě**, **Kreativa** / **Produktová kreativa** (eshop), **Obsah — plán** (local GBP posts), **Distribuce** (content). Do NOT script — the Character finds the fastest path from "I need a week of content" to a scheduled batch. Friction, jargon, or robotic output that would make them not repeat it is a finding.

## Grounding to check (L1)
Do the caption/creative/post prompts receive the real product/service (name, benefit, brand voice/accent), or generic placeholders? Score grounding N/M per AI surface. A "sounds like nobody" risk is a senior-quality finding.

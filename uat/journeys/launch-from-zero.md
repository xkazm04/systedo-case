## Trigger (why now)
The Character has a landing page and a launch date but no traffic, customers, or budget. They open the app to build early visibility and to see whether it's useful *before* they have any data.

## Definition of done (their POV)
- Modules that don't need history — keyword/demand, content ideas, social — produce **useful output from zero**.
- Every data-dependent module they open has a **helpful empty state** (teaches / redirects), never a blank screen, a broken chart, or a dead-end.
- They leave with a concrete **pre-/post-launch visibility plan** (queries + content + channels) assembled in one sitting.
- Nothing forces a budget or an existing audience to reach first value.

## Out of scope
- Validating real market demand externally (only the app's in-product signal is judged).
- Post-launch performance analysis (no data yet by definition).

## Discovery hints
Entry point(s): **/app** (create/enter the project), then the project **Přehled** and the zero-data modules (**Klíčová slova**, **Obsahový engine**, **Sociální sítě**), plus deliberately opening a data-hungry module (**Výkon**, **CAC→LTV**, **Knihovna vzorů**) to test its empty state. Do NOT script — a dead-end empty state or a module that's useless without data is exactly the finding.

## Grounding to check (L1)
For each zero-data module: does it degrade gracefully (useful default / teaching empty state) or dead-end? For AI surfaces reachable pre-launch, is output grounded in the offering the user *can* provide, or does it demand data they don't have?
